D6.3 Reflection of Light#

D6.3.1 Learning Goals#

By the end of this lesson, students should be able to:

  • State the law of reflection.

  • Construct accurate ray diagrams for reflection.

  • Explain reflection using ray optics principles.

  • Predict the path of a light ray reflecting off flat and curved surfaces.

D6.3.2 Law of Reflection#

The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection:

\[ \theta_i = \theta_r \]

Where:

  • \( \theta_i \) is the angle between the incident ray and the normal (perpendicular to the surface).

  • \( \theta_r \) is the angle between the reflected ray and the normal.

This law holds for:

  • Flat (plane) mirrors

  • Smooth curved surfaces (locally approximated as flat)

The normal line is always drawn perpendicular to the surface at the point of incidence.

D6.3.3 Drawing Ray Diagrams for Plane Mirrors#

Steps:

  1. Draw the incident ray approaching the surface.

  2. At the point of incidence, draw the normal.

  3. Measure the angle \( \theta_i \) from the incident ray to the normal.

  4. Draw the reflected ray such that \( \theta_r = \theta_i \).

Conventions:

  • Use arrows to indicate the direction of ray travel.

  • Label all angles and rays clearly.

D6.3.4 Reflection from Curved Surfaces#

We can apply the same law of reflection locally to curved mirrors:

  • Treat each small part of the surface as if it were flat.

  • At the point of incidence, draw the normal (perpendicular to the tangent).

  • Use the law \( \theta_i = \theta_r \).

D6.3.7 Summary#

  • Light reflects from surfaces according to the rule: \( \theta_i = \theta_r \).

  • The law of reflection applies to all surfaces, flat or curved, when defined locally.

  • Reflection is a cornerstone of ray optics and forms the basis for more complex optical systems.

Box 1: Predicting the Reflection Angle

A floor-to-ceiling vertical mirror is mounted on a wall, and a laser beam strikes it at a point \(35.0\) cm above the floor. The upward angle of incidence is \(35^\circ\) with respect to the normal.

Tasks:

  1. Sketch the situation: include the floor, the mirror, the laser ray, and the normal.

  2. What is the angle of reflection?

  3. If the opposite vertical wall is \(5.0\) m from the mirror, where will the reflected beam hit that wall? (How high above the floor?)

Box 2: Misleading Angle — Laser on a Tabletop Mirror

A laser pointer emits a beam that strikes a horizontal mirror at an angle of \(20^\circ\) relative to the surface of the mirror.

Tasks:

  1. Carefully sketch the situation.

  2. Determine the angle of reflection.

  3. If the reflected beam hits a wall 2.0 meters away horizontally, how high above the mirror’s surface will the laser spot be?
